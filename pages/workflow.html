Workflow

Step-by-step methodology from data acquisition to WebGIS publication.

Methodological Overview

Our methodology integrates multi-source geospatial data and geoprocessing techniques using QGIS, GeoServer and OpenLayers. The workflow analyses air quality trends, land cover transitions and population exposure in Serbia.

01

Data Acquisition

CAMS European Air Quality Reanalysis data were collected for NO₂, PM2.5 and PM10. ESRI 10m Annual Land Cover maps were used for 2021 and 2023, while WorldPop 2023 population counts supported the exposure analysis. Serbian administrative boundaries were used for clipping and zonal statistics.

02

Preprocessing

All spatial layers were reprojected to EPSG:4326 and clipped to Serbia. CAMS rasters, land cover maps and population layers were harmonized to support consistent raster calculations and spatial overlays.

03

Air Quality Mapping

Annual Mean Aggregation Change maps were produced between 2021 and 2023 for NO₂, PM2.5 and PM10. Positive values indicate increasing concentration, while negative values indicate decreasing concentration.

04

Land Cover Change

A land cover transition raster was generated using the expression ("LC_2021" × 100) + "LC_2023". This allowed stable areas, gains and losses of selected land cover classes to be identified.

05

Zonal Statistics

Mean, minimum and maximum pollutant concentrations were calculated for administrative zones. These statistics support comparison between regions and help identify pollution hotspots.

06

Population Exposure

WorldPop 2023 population data were classified into five classes. Population classes were combined with pollution severity classes to create bivariate exposure maps.

07

GeoServer and WebGIS

Final raster and vector outputs were uploaded to GeoServer as WMS/WFS layers and visualized in an OpenLayers WebGIS with base maps, controls, legends and layer switching.
